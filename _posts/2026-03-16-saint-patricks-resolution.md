I got rejected form an internship today. Rejections happen all the time, but this one stung bad. I was sent the rejection email four days ago in my junk
inbox but hadn't seen it in my spam folder. So today, I embarrassed myself by sending a follow-up email to an employer that had already rejected me. After I'd sent the follow-up but before I recieved the  Between the time that I sent that email and
the time I got the polite, humiliating response from Outreach Astronomer Mallory, I went
to class. There I discovered that my employee, a classmate who works for me, had been chosen for the internship
instead of myself. 
God it was just so unbearable. This classmate is truly smart and
talented, and I have nothing against them. I'm happy that they'll get the chance to work
there. But I'm pretty frustrated with myself. I knew that my chances of getting this job
were slim, but I was actually dumb enough and bad enough at managing my time that I did
not apply to a single other internship. I just hail mary-ed one application and half-filled
the rest before giving up on them and thinking that somehow that plan would work out.
Jesus fucking christ.

I think I need to change how I'm doing life in at least some major way.
I've wasted so many hours of so many days and I think I have delusions about how things are going to work out just fine. I want to start making some small changes day by day. This
was my New Year's Resolution, but I guess now it's my Saint Patrick's Resolution: I am
going to write here, just for fun, and I'm going to make it something I can be proud of. Having it here makes it
"published," and a place where someone else could see it if they wanted to. It go in a very personal direction or a more professional, look-at-all-my-accomplishments direction, but either way I just need a passion project that is entirely my own.
